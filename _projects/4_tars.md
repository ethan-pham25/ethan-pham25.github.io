Problem:    Design, implement, and test an **autonomous animatronic** from popular science fiction. 

Solution:   **Lead 12 students** across mechanical, electrical, and software via general and subteam meetings, collaborative CAD on **Onshape**, and quarterly design reviews. Design and manufacture lightweight aluminum frame and **modular 4-DOF robotic arm.**  

Results:    Delivered master CAD and initial prototypes **on time** and **under budget** in Fall and Winter quarters. Finishing assembly for integrated tests at end of the Spring 2026 quarter.

During Fall quarter, I used **Onshape** for computer-aided design of TARS's frame and subarm. I also helped coordinate the overall design and **architecture of the software and electrical systems**, and quickly printed a (mostly) working prototype of the robot's gripper.

Throughout Winter quarter, I worked on manufacturing the frame and integrating electrical components with my 3d-printed subarm. I **iterated** on the gripper and subarm joints to make them smoother and easier to assemble/dissasemble.

I also worked with the electrical team to refine requirements for how the mechanical and electrical systems would interface (power budget, amperage and voltage, mounting placements, etc.).
